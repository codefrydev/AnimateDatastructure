1 What are we actually comparing? (The "Suffix")

The matrix does NOT compare single letters. It compares the rest of the word starting from a specific slot. Let's use the word "apple".

Start Slot 0:
a p p l e
Start Slot 1:
p p l e
Start Slot 2:
p l e

2 How does the Matrix calculate a clue?

Let's look at how the computer calculates Clue[1][2]. It stacks the word from Slot 1 and the word from Slot 2, and reads left-to-right until they stop matching.

Slot 1:
p p l e
Slot 2:
p l e
They share exactly 1 letter (the p) before hitting a mismatch (p vs l).
Therefore, the computer writes down: Clue[1][2] = 1

3 The Reverse Engineering (The "Aha!" Moment)

Now, let's play the game backwards. We don't know the word. We just have blank spaces _ _ _ _ _ and the computer gives you a clue: Clue [0][3] = 2.

This clue guarantees that when we stack Slot 0 and Slot 3, they will share exactly 2 letters. Look at what this visually forces to happen:

Because the clue is 2, the boxes stacked perfectly on top of each other MUST be identical letters!

  • 1 The 1st boxes must match: Slot 0 == Slot 3
  • 2 The 2nd boxes must match: Slot 1 == Slot 4

The Ultimate Rule for our Algorithm:

Our algorithm builds the secret word by guessing one letter at a time, moving from left to right.

If we see a Matrix clue that is greater than 0, we instantly know that those two Suffixes share AT LEAST 1 letter. Therefore, their very first boxes are guaranteed to be twins!
